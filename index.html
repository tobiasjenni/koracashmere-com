Our Heritage

From Goat to Garment

In the Kathmandu Valley, cashmere weaving is not a profession — it is inheritance. For three generations, our artisans have transformed the finest Himalayan fibers into heirlooms of warmth.

Each piece begins with the Changthangi goat, grazing at altitudes above 4,000 meters. The raw cashmere is hand-sorted, washed in mountain streams, and spun on traditional charkhas. Every stitch carries the memory of hands that have been weaving for a lifetime.

No machines. No shortcuts. Just the slow, deliberate rhythm of human hands — the same rhythm that has defined Nepalese craft for centuries.

Our Principles

Rooted in respect — for people, place, and process

Ethically Sourced

Our cashmere comes from free-ranging Changthangi goats in the high Himalayas. We work directly with herder cooperatives, ensuring fair trade and traceability from mountain to loom.

Handcrafted

Every garment is entirely handmade — from spinning and dyeing to weaving and finishing. Three generations of Kathmandu artisans pour their knowledge into every stitch.

Timeless Design

We design for decades, not seasons. Clean lines, earth-toned palettes, and enduring silhouettes that grow softer with every wear — pieces meant to be passed down.
