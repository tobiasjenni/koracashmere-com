The Material

Why Himalayan Cashmere

At 4,000 metres, the air thins and the cold deepens. To survive, the Changthangi goat grows a second coat — an underdown so fine, so insulative, that no machine has ever replicated it.

Each spring, when the goats naturally shed, herders hand-comb the precious fibres. One goat yields only 150 grams per year — barely enough for a single scarf. This scarcity is not a limitation. It is the foundation of everything we make.

The Craft

Hands That Remember

In the Kathmandu Valley, cashmere weaving is not a profession — it is inheritance. For three generations, our artisans have transformed the finest Himalayan fibres into heirlooms of warmth.

From the charkha wheel to the wooden loom, every stage is guided by hands that have practiced this rhythm for decades. No machines. No shortcuts. Just the slow, deliberate cadence of human touch — the same rhythm that has defined Nepalese craft for centuries.

Our Principles

How We Measure Ourselves

Luxury without conscience is empty. These three commitments guide every decision in our workshop.

Ethically Sourced

Our cashmere comes from free-ranging Changthangi goats in the high Himalayas. We work directly with herder cooperatives, ensuring fair trade and full traceability from mountain to loom.

Handcrafted

Every garment is entirely handmade — from spinning and dyeing to weaving and finishing. Three generations of Kathmandu artisans pour their knowledge into every stitch.

Timeless Design

We design for decades, not seasons. Clean lines, earth-toned palettes, and enduring silhouettes that grow softer with every wear — pieces meant to be passed down.
