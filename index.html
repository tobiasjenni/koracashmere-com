The Hands

Three Generations at the Loom

In the Kathmandu Valley, weaving is not a profession — it is inheritance. Grandmothers teach daughters, who teach their daughters. Every Kora piece carries the accumulated wisdom of hands that have been weaving for centuries.

The Fiber

Born at 4,000 Meters

The Changthangi goat survives temperatures of −40°C by growing an undercoat finer than any other natural fiber — 12 to 15 microns in diameter. One goat yields only 150 grams per year. A single scarf requires the annual harvest of three goats.
