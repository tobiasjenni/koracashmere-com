Our Story

Three generations of Kathmandu artisans

The Origin

Born of the Himalaya

The Himalayan cashmere goat has roamed the high plateaus of Nepal for centuries. In the Kathmandu Valley, three generations of our family have transformed this precious fibre into garments that carry the warmth of the mountains.

It begins at altitudes above 4,000 meters, where the Changthangi goat grows its fine undercoat against the bitter cold. Each spring, the raw cashmere is hand-combed — never sheared — preserving the animal's well-being and the integrity of each precious strand.

From these remote highlands to the valley looms of Kathmandu, every fibre carries with it the story of a landscape that has shaped our craft for as long as anyone can remember.

The Process

From Fibre to Finished Piece

Every KORA garment passes through four generations of care — each step guided by hand, each stage a tradition refined over decades.

Step 01

Sourcing

Raw cashmere is hand-combed from Changthangi goats in the high Himalayas. Only the finest undercoat fibres are selected, ensuring exceptional softness from the very beginning.

Step 02

Spinning

On traditional charkha wheels, the raw fibres are spun into delicate yet durable yarn. This slow, rhythmic process preserves the natural loft and breathability of the cashmere.

Step 03

Weaving

On hand-operated wooden looms, our master weavers transform yarn into fabric. Each row is pulled tight by hand, creating a dense, durable weave that will last for generations.

Step 04

Finishing

Each garment is hand-washed in soft water, gently stretched to shape, and inspected stitch by stitch. Only when our Quality Master is satisfied does it receive the KORA label.

Our Values

Rooted in respect — for people, place, and process

Ethical Sourcing

Our cashmere comes from free-ranging Changthangi goats in the high Himalayas. We work directly with herder cooperatives, ensuring fair trade and full traceability from mountain to loom.

Women Empowerment

Our workshop is led and staffed primarily by women artisans. We provide fair wages, flexible hours, skill development, and a supportive environment where craft traditions are passed on.

Zero Waste

Every scrap of cashmere is repurposed. Fibre offcuts become felted accessories, yarn remnants are woven into patchwork throws, and even the dust is composted. Nothing goes to landfill.

Fair Wages

We believe that true luxury cannot exist without dignity. Our artisans earn well above fair-trade standards, with health benefits, paid leave, and a share in the company's success.
